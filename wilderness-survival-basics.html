10 Wilderness Survival Tips

The woods don’t care about your feelings. Out there, it’s you, the elements, and whatever’s hunting. Here's how to go primal and stay alive.

  1. Build Shelter First: Before food, before fire — get out of the wind and rain.
  2. Fire = Life: Master 3 ways to light one. Wet wood? Figure it out. Or freeze.
  3. Signal the Sky: Bright tarp, mirror, smoke. Get seen if you want out.
  4. Don’t Drink Every Stream: Boil or filter. Beaver fever is real and brutal.
  5. Stay Put if Lost: Wandering equals dying. Make yourself visible. Think, don’t panic.
  6. Sharp = Survival: Your knife is your lifeline. Keep it ready, keep it clean.
  7. Move Smart: Conserve energy. Scout daylight routes. Don’t “just walk east.”
  8. Track Time & Temp: Know sunset. Know signs of hypothermia. Plan backwards.
  9. Food Can Wait: Water matters more. Hunger won’t kill you in 3 days. Thirst will.
  10. Respect Nature, But Dominate It: You’re not prey. Not if you prep right.

You’re not lost. You’re just off the grid — on purpose.
