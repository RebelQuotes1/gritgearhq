10 Power Outage Survival Tips

When the grid goes dark, most people panic. You? You're prepared. Here's how to ride out a blackout without losing your mind — or your meat.

  1. Light It Right: Solar lanterns, candles, headlamps. Don't rely on one source.
  2. Battery Bank Army: Charge everything in advance. Rotate use, keep one fully juiced.
  3. Don’t Open the Fridge: Every peek is a 10-degree drop. Think before you grab that soda.
  4. Boil Water, Not Nerves: Know how to purify water manually. Blackouts often mess with city systems.
  5. Solar Power Wins: Get panels or a solar generator. Silent and endlessly useful.
  6. Cash on Hand: ATMs won’t work. Digital payments are dead. Old-school green is king.
  7. Portable Radio = Intel: A crank radio gives you news, weather, warnings. No guesswork.
  8. Secure Doors and Windows: Darkness breeds crime. Fortify the homestead quietly.
  9. Cook Smart: Camp stoves, grills, even a tin can rocket stove. Eat hot or stay cold.
  10. Keep Morale Up: Boredom kills faster than hunger. Cards, books, stories — anything but fear.

Power comes and goes. Grit? That’s permanent.
