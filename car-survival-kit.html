10 Items for Car Survival

If you get stranded in your car, you're in a steel coffin — unless you’ve packed smart. Here's what every vehicle should have before you end up in the ditch with no signal and freezing rain.

  1. Wool Blanket: Cheap. Reliable. Life-saving warmth when the heater's gone.
  2. Multi-tool: Cuts, twists, unscrews, stabs. All-in-one lifeline.
  3. First Aid Kit: Bleed control, pain relief, gauze. Don’t forget trauma shears.
  4. Water Pouches: Freeze-proof, compact. Enough to hold you 48 hours.
  5. Flashlight & Batteries: Ditch your phone light. Use something meant for real work.
  6. Protein Bars: Won’t spoil in the heat. Won’t freeze in the cold. Energy, fast.
  7. Folding Shovel: Snow, mud, or burying a mistake — just kidding. Maybe.
  8. Phone Charger + Power Bank: You’ll need juice when it matters. Charge up every few days.
  9. Reflective Triangle + Flares: Be seen before you're crushed on a highway shoulder.
  10. Duct Tape: Fix hoses, bandage wounds, even make snowshoes. It's a miracle roll.

Don’t just survive the road — dominate it.
